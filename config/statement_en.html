Brandubh

An ancient Celtic game of strategy. A small group of defenders must help their King move to safety, while a larger force of attackers tries to capture him.

  The Goal

Play either the Attackers or the Defenders in a game of Brandubh (a variant of Hnefatafl). You must fulfill your side's victory conditions before your opponent does.

Note: The Defenders control the King as well as the regular defending pieces.

  Rules

For complete details, you can consult the official rules PDF. Note the following deviations for this CodinGame adaptation:

  • The official rules use the terms "black" and "white" instead of "Attackers" and "Defenders".
  • Point 10 (a full game is played in two sets) is irrelevant. Every CodinGame match is a single game.
  • The concepts of a turn limit and score don't exist in the official rules but are used here to prevent infinite games.

Board & Coordinates: The game is played on a 7x7 grid. The initial board state is provided to your program during the first turn's input. The coordinate system starts at (0, 0) in the top-left corner. The x-coordinate increases to the right, and the y-coordinate increases downwards.

Movement: All pieces move orthogonally (like a rook in chess) any number of empty squares. Pieces cannot jump over other pieces.

The Throne and Corners: The center square (3,3) is the Throne. The 4 corner squares are the escape zones. Only the King can stop on the Throne or the corners. Other pieces can pass over the Throne if it is empty, but cannot land on it.

Hostile Pieces: The opponent's pieces are considered hostile pieces. The Throne and the corners also act as hostile pieces for the purpose of capturing.

The Throne's Hostility: The Throne is always hostile to Attackers. However, it is only hostile to Defenders if the King is not currently standing on it.

Capturing: A piece (attacker or defender) is captured if it is flanked on two opposite sides by hostile pieces.

Capturing the King: The King is captured like any other piece, except when he is on the Throne or immediately adjacent to it. If the King is on the Throne, he must be surrounded on all 4 sides to be captured. If he is adjacent to the Throne, he must be surrounded on the 3 remaining sides.

Game End & Score: The game ends immediately when a victory or defeat condition is met. The winner receives a score of 1000 - current_turn, while the loser receives 0. If neither side wins after 200 turns (100 rounds), a draw is declared and both players receive a score of 0.

Source Code: The source code for this game (Referee) is available on GitLab.

Victory Conditions
  • Defenders: The King reaches one of the 4 corner squares.
  • Attackers: The King is captured, or all remaining defenders are encircled and cannot move.
Defeat Conditions
  • You have no valid moves available.
  • You repeat the exact same board position 3 times (3-fold repetition rule).
  • Your program provides an invalid move.
  • Your program times out.

  Game Input

Input for one game turn

Line 1: role - an integer representing your role (0 for Attackers, 1 for Defenders).

Next 7 lines: A 7-character string representing the board state. Characters can be:

  • .: Empty square
  • A: Attacker piece
  • D: Defender piece
  • K: The King
Output
A single line containing your move in the format: MOVE x1 y1 x2 y2.
  • x1, y1: The starting coordinates of the piece you want to move.
  • x2, y2: The destination coordinates for that piece.
For example: MOVE 3 1 3 0 to move the piece from (3,1) to (3,0).
Constraints
Allotted response time to output is ≤ 100 ms for the first turn, and ≤ 50 ms for subsequent turns.
